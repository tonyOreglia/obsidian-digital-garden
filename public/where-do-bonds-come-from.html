Where Do Bonds Come From?

  • Primary and Secondary Bond Markets
    • Primary
      • Small, newly issued bonds
    • Secondary
      • investors buy/sell bonds that have already been issued
      • larger

How bonds are issued

  • Issued by some entity
  • Underwriting company usually helps writing bond details
    • Maturity, coupon rate (interest rate, usually semi-annual), face value / par value
  • coupon rate determined by entities credit rating, competing coupon rates
  • Only newly issued bonds can be bought at face value
  • As interest rates fall, bond prices rise and vice versa
  • coupon rate real value does not change when bond is sold at different value than face value
  • Yield to maturity = adjusted rate of return based on current price, coupon rate, and time to maturity
    • This value helps with comparison of bonds
  • When comparing bonds with similar yield to maturity; looks for one with a higher rating
